Research

Goddess Worship and Parental Investment: Evidence from Navratri and Vaccination Rates in India
Working Paper
This paper examines how exposure to cultural festivals celebrating female deities affects parental investments in daughters' health. Using administrative data on vaccination records and the timing of Navratri—a major Hindu festival honoring goddesses—I find that girls born shortly after the festival receive approximately 6% more vaccinations compared to girls born before the festival. This finding suggests that cultural exposure to positive representations of femininity can influence gender-discriminatory behavior in parental investments. The results contribute to our understanding of how cultural transmission affects economic outcomes and gender inequality.
